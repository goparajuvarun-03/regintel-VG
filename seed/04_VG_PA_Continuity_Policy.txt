§ 1.0 — Purpose

This policy governs how VG Health Plan handles prior authorization
(PA) decisions when new members transition into the plan from another
Medicare Advantage organization, from Original Medicare, or from
Medicaid managed care. Its objective is to prevent disruption of
ongoing medically necessary treatments during the transition period.


§ 2.0 — Scope

This policy applies to VG Health Plan Medicare Advantage products,
including HMO, PPO, D-SNP, and C-SNP lines of business, in all
contracted service areas. It is binding on VG staff, delegated
utilization management vendors, and contracted providers.


§ 3.0 — Definitions

(a) "Transitioning member" means a member whose effective enrollment
date with VG Health Plan is within the prior thirty (30) days, and
who was previously enrolled with another insurer or with Original
Medicare.

(b) "Prior plan PA decision" means a documented prior authorization
decision issued by the member's previous insurer for a service or
medication that remains medically necessary at the time of transition.

(c) "Treatment plan" means a documented course of clinical care
defined by an attending or specialty provider.


§ 4.0 — Continuity Requirements

§ 4.1 — General Continuity Standard

When a new member enrolls in VG Health Plan, the plan will accept
existing prior authorization decisions made by the prior plan for
ongoing treatments, subject to the limits below.

§ 4.2 — Continuity Window

VG Health Plan honors prior plan PA decisions for thirty (30) days
following the member's effective enrollment date. After day thirty
(30), the member's care must be re-authorized under VG's standard
PA process.

§ 4.3 — Exception for Active Cancer Treatment

For active oncology treatment in progress at the time of transition,
the plan extends the continuity window to ninety (90) days. The
member's oncologist must submit treatment plan documentation to
VG's clinical review team within fifteen (15) days of the
member's enrollment.

§ 4.4 — Documentation Requirements

VG's clinical team requires the following to honor a prior plan
PA decision:
  (i) the prior plan's PA approval letter or claim history;
  (ii) the member's relevant clinical notes;
  (iii) the prescribing provider's NPI;
  (iv) the treatment's CPT or HCPCS code.

§ 4.5 — Reporting

The Utilization Management department submits an annual report to
the Compliance Committee documenting:
  - total transitioning members;
  - total prior plan PA decisions honored;
  - exceptions granted under § 4.3;
  - denials and member appeals.


§ 5.0 — Denial and Appeal Procedures

§ 5.1 — Denial of Continuity Request

If the clinical review team determines that a prior plan PA decision
will not be honored, the member receives a written denial letter
within ten (10) business days. The letter explains the reason for
denial and outlines appeal rights under 42 CFR § 422.566.

§ 5.2 — Standard Appeal

Members may file a standard appeal within sixty (60) days of the
denial letter. VG's appeals team renders a decision within
thirty (30) days.

§ 5.3 — Expedited Appeal

In urgent cases, members or providers may request an expedited
appeal. VG renders an expedited decision within seventy-two (72)
hours of receipt.


§ 6.0 — Roles and Responsibilities

  - Member Services: identifies transitioning members at enrollment.
  - Utilization Management: reviews prior plan PA documentation.
  - Clinical Review Team: makes continuity decisions.
  - Appeals & Grievances: processes denials and appeals.
  - Compliance Committee: reviews annual report and metrics.


KNOWN GAPS (internal note, not part of policy)

The Compliance team is aware of the following items requiring
review against current CMS guidance:
  - § 4.2 thirty-day window may need extension based on CMS-4201
    Final Rule.
  - § 4.5 annual reporting cadence is under review against the
    Final Rule's quarterly requirement.
  - § 4.3 exception scope is currently limited to oncology and
    may need broader treatment-plan language.
