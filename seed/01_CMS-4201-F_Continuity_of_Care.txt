CMS-4201-F · MEDICARE ADVANTAGE CONTINUITY OF CARE FINAL RULE
Issuing body: Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS)
Regulation ID: CMS-4201-F
Effective date: 2026-01-15
Status: Final Rule
Publication: Federal Register, Vol. 90, No. 215


§ 1.0 — Purpose

This Final Rule establishes minimum standards for continuity of care
when Medicare Advantage (MA) enrollees transition between MA
organizations or from Original Medicare. The Rule replaces and
extends the previously published Proposed Rule (CMS-4201-P, March 2025).


§ 2.0 — Scope

This Rule applies to all Medicare Advantage organizations, Special
Needs Plans (SNPs), and Medicare-Medicaid Plans (MMPs) operating
under Part C of the Social Security Act. It is binding on contract
year 2026 and forward.


§ 3.0 — Definitions

(a) "Transitioning member" means a Medicare Advantage enrollee whose
plan-of-record changes due to election, disenrollment, special
enrollment period, or annual coordinated election period.

(b) "Prior plan PA decision" means a prior authorization determination
made by the member's previous MA organization, Original Medicare
fee-for-service contractor, or Medicaid managed care plan.

(c) "Clinically established treatment plan" means a documented course
of treatment ordered by a qualified clinician and supported by a
diagnosis recorded in the member's medical record at the time of
transition.


§ 4.0 — Continuity Window Requirements

§ 4.1 — General Standard

Every Medicare Advantage organization shall honor prior plan PA
decisions for transitioning members under the requirements of this
section. Honoring a prior plan PA decision means accepting the
clinical determination without requiring resubmission, additional
documentation, or step-therapy reapplication.

§ 4.2 — Minimum Window

A Medicare Advantage organization shall honor prior plan PA decisions
for a period of NOT LESS THAN ninety (90) days following the member's
effective enrollment date with the receiving plan.

NOTE: This requirement expands the proposed thirty (30) day window
published in CMS-4201-P. CMS finds that ninety (90) days better
protects enrollee continuity during transitions while remaining
operationally feasible.

§ 4.3 — Clinically Established Treatment Plans

Where a member is undergoing a clinically established treatment plan
at the time of transition, the receiving MA organization shall honor
the prior plan PA decision for the FULL DURATION of that treatment
plan, regardless of the ninety (90) day minimum window in § 4.2.

For chronic conditions requiring ongoing therapy (oncology,
nephrology, behavioral health, post-surgical rehabilitation), the
receiving plan shall not require reassessment until the originally
prescribed end-date of the treatment plan.


§ 5.0 — Denial Standards

§ 5.1 — Denial Notice Requirement

If the receiving MA organization determines that a prior plan PA
decision shall not be honored (for example, because the underlying
service is not a covered benefit), the organization shall issue a
written denial notice to the member within five (5) business days.

§ 5.2 — Required Content of Denial Notice

The denial notice shall include:
  (i) the specific clinical and administrative basis for the denial;
  (ii) the right to appeal under 42 CFR § 422.566;
  (iii) the procedure for filing an expedited appeal;
  (iv) contact information for the State Health Insurance
       Assistance Program (SHIP) and Medicare ombudsman.

§ 5.3 — Appeal Rights

Members denied continuity-of-care coverage under this Rule shall have
the right to file an expedited appeal within seventy-two (72) hours
of receiving the denial notice. The MA organization shall render an
appeal decision within seventy-two (72) hours of receipt.


§ 6.0 — Reporting

§ 6.1 — Quarterly Compliance Report

Each Medicare Advantage organization shall submit a quarterly
compliance report to CMS using the prescribed CMS template within
thirty (30) calendar days of the close of each calendar quarter.

NOTE: The Proposed Rule required annual reporting. The Final Rule
moves to quarterly to enable closer CMS oversight.

§ 6.2 — Required Data Elements

The quarterly report shall contain, at minimum:
  (i) total transitioning members during the reporting period;
  (ii) total prior plan PA decisions honored;
  (iii) total denials issued and reasons;
  (iv) appeal volume and disposition;
  (v) clinically established treatment plans extended beyond
      ninety (90) days, by clinical category.


§ 7.0 — Enforcement

Failure to comply with this Rule may result in:
  (a) civil monetary penalty under 42 CFR § 422.752 up to $25,000
      per occurrence;
  (b) corrective action plan with quarterly attestations to CMS;
  (c) intermediate sanctions including suspension of new
      enrollments under 42 CFR § 422.752(c)(1);
  (d) referral to the HHS Office of Inspector General where
      pattern violations indicate program abuse.
