§ 1.0 — Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure defines the day-to-day operational
steps for maintaining the VG Health Plan provider network,
including provider data verification, directory publication, and
member-facing accuracy controls.


§ 2.0 — Scope

This SOP applies to all Provider Network Operations staff,
contracted credentialing vendors, and Provider Data Services.
It governs the provider directory published at
providers.vghealthplan.com and the printed directory mailed to
new enrollees within ten (10) business days of enrollment.


§ 3.0 — Definitions

(a) "Directory entry" means a single record in the provider master
file representing one provider at one practice location.

(b) "Verification" means the process of confirming with the
provider that the directory entry's data is current and correct.

(c) "Refresh" means publication of the verified provider master file
to the public-facing directory.


§ 4.0 — Provider Verification Workflow

§ 4.1 — Verification Cadence

Each directory entry is verified at least once every one hundred
eighty (180) days. Verification is initiated by the Provider Data
Services team using automated outreach (email + portal) and follows
up with phone outreach if no response within fourteen (14) days.

§ 4.2 — Verification Data Elements

The verification request asks the provider to confirm:
  (i) practice location address;
  (ii) telephone number;
  (iii) accepting-new-patients status;
  (iv) languages spoken;
  (v) hospital affiliations.

§ 4.3 — Wheelchair and ADA Accessibility

Wheelchair accessibility and ADA compliance flags are captured at
provider credentialing but are not part of the routine verification
cycle. Updates are accepted via the provider portal at any time.

§ 4.4 — Unresponsive Providers

A provider who fails to respond to two (2) consecutive verification
cycles is flagged as "stale" in the master file. Stale entries are
escalated to the Provider Relations team for direct outreach.


§ 5.0 — Directory Publication

§ 5.1 — Refresh Frequency

The public-facing directory is refreshed monthly, on the first
business day of each month, with all verified updates received
since the prior refresh.

§ 5.2 — Print Directory

The print directory mailed to new enrollees is generated on the
first business day of each month from the same master file. It is
distributed by the Member Communications team within ten (10)
business days of new enrollee identification.

§ 5.3 — Online Directory Display

Each entry displays a "last verified" date. Entries verified more
than one hundred eighty (180) days ago display a yellow caution
banner advising members to call the provider's office to confirm
availability.


§ 6.0 — Member Complaint Handling

§ 6.1 — Inaccuracy Reports

Members may report directory inaccuracies via the Member Services
line or the online complaint form. Inaccuracy reports are routed to
Provider Data Services for investigation within five (5) business
days.

§ 6.2 — Resolution Timeline

Resolution of an inaccuracy report is targeted at fifteen (15)
business days from receipt. Resolution may include directory entry
update, removal of the entry, or escalation to Provider Relations.

§ 6.3 — Complaint Log

A complaint log is maintained by Provider Data Services and is
reviewed monthly by the Provider Network Operations Director.
Complaints are retained for three (3) years from date of receipt.


§ 7.0 — Network Adequacy Monitoring

§ 7.1 — Time-and-Distance Analysis

Network Operations runs a time-and-distance analysis annually,
no later than December 31 of each year, using GeoNetworks software.
Results are compared to current CMS standards and any gaps are
reported to the Network Strategy team.

§ 7.2 — Behavioral Health Specialty Tracking

Behavioral health provider density is tracked separately on a
quarterly basis. The tracking dashboard is reviewed by the
Behavioral Health Operations Lead.


§ 8.0 — Roles and Responsibilities

  - Provider Data Services: executes verification workflow.
  - Provider Relations: outreach to unresponsive providers.
  - Member Communications: publishes the directory.
  - Network Operations: monitors adequacy and complaints.
  - Provider Network Operations Director: monthly complaint review.


KNOWN GAPS (internal note, not part of SOP)

  - § 4.1 verification cadence may need to move from 180 days to
    90 days based on CMS-2439 Final Rule.
  - § 5.1 monthly refresh may need to move to a 5-business-day
    cadence to meet directory accuracy standards.
  - § 6.3 complaint retention currently three (3) years; CMS-2439
    requires not less than six (6) years.
  - § 7.1 annual adequacy attestation cadence under review.
