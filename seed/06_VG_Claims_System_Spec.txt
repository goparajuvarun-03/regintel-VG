§ 1.0 — Purpose

This specification documents the design, business rules, and
operational behavior of the VG-CLAIMS-CORE claims adjudication
system, which processes all Medicare Advantage Part C claims
submitted to VG Health Plan.


§ 2.0 — Scope

This specification applies to:
  - VG-CLAIMS-CORE production system, version 1.7;
  - integration points with eligibility, network, and PA systems;
  - downstream interfaces to payment processing and provider
    explanation-of-benefits generation.


§ 3.0 — System Architecture Overview

§ 3.1 — Components

The system consists of:
  - Ingest service (EDI 837 inbound, NPI-validated);
  - Eligibility validator (real-time call to MEMBER-DB);
  - Coverage rules engine (RULE-PACK v4.12);
  - Medical-necessity review queue (manual + ML-assisted);
  - Adjudication decision engine;
  - Payment file generator (835 outbound).

§ 3.2 — Throughput

The system processes approximately one million two hundred thousand
(1,200,000) claims per month. Peak hourly volume is approximately
six thousand (6,000) claims.


§ 4.0 — Business Rules

§ 4.1 — Timely Filing Window

The system rejects in-network provider claims received more than
one hundred eighty (180) calendar days after the date of service,
with rejection reason code "TFL01" (Timely Filing Limit Exceeded).
Out-of-network claims have a one-year (365 day) window.

§ 4.2 — Clean Claim Adjudication SLA

The system targets adjudication of clean claims within forty-five
(45) calendar days of receipt. This SLA is enforced by the
adjudication queue prioritizer.

§ 4.3 — Non-Clean-Claim Handling

When a claim is identified as non-clean (missing required field,
failed validation, missing attachment), the system generates a
request for additional information letter. The letter is mailed
within thirty (30) calendar days of identification, and the
adjudication clock is paused until the additional information is
received.

§ 4.4 — Coordination of Benefits

For claims requiring coordination of benefits with another primary
payer, the system holds the claim in a "COB-PENDING" queue until
the primary payer's EOB is received. The system does not impose a
timely-filing rejection on COB-PENDING claims within the COB
holding period.


§ 5.0 — Interest and Penalty Calculation

§ 5.1 — Late Payment Interest

The system does not currently calculate or post interest on late
clean-claim payments. Provider Relations manually reviews any
provider complaint regarding late payment and issues interest
adjustments on a case-by-case basis.

§ 5.2 — Interest Rate Source

There is no system-level field for the Treasury interest rate.
Manual interest adjustments use the Treasury rate published on the
date of adjustment.


§ 6.0 — Reporting Interfaces

§ 6.1 — Daily Claims Aging Extract

The system generates a daily claims-aging extract for the Finance
team. The extract reports total claims by aging bucket
(0-30, 31-60, 61-90, >90 days).

§ 6.2 — Monthly Compliance Extract

The system generates a monthly compliance extract for the
Compliance team. The extract reports total denials, denial reasons,
appeals filed, and appeal outcomes.

§ 6.3 — Star Ratings Data Push

The system does not currently publish claims-adjudication
performance data to the Star Ratings data feed. Performance data
is reported manually by the Compliance team on an annual basis.


§ 7.0 — Integration Points

§ 7.1 — MEMBER-DB Eligibility

The system calls MEMBER-DB in real time for eligibility
verification on every inbound claim. Call timeout is set at three
(3) seconds. Timeout claims are routed to the manual review queue.

§ 7.2 — Network Verification

The system calls PROVIDER-DB to verify provider network status at
the date of service. The verification result is logged with the
claim for audit purposes.

§ 7.3 — Prior Authorization Lookup

The system calls PA-DB to confirm that any required prior
authorization is on file. Claims with missing required PA are
denied with reason code "PA01" (Required PA Not Found).


§ 8.0 — Audit and Compliance

§ 8.1 — Audit Log Retention

Adjudication decisions are retained in the audit log for ten (10)
years from date of service.

§ 8.2 — User Activity Logging

User activity in the adjudication system is logged with userID,
timestamp, claim ID, and action taken. Logs are retained for seven
(7) years.


KNOWN LIMITATIONS (internal note, not part of spec)

  - § 4.2 clean-claim SLA is 45 days, currently exceeding CMS
    standard of 30 days for clean-claim adjudication.
  - § 5.1 no automated interest calculation on late payments;
    manual process not scalable to expected volume.
  - § 6.3 no automated Star Ratings data feed; CMS-MLN-7521
    references claims-adjudication performance as Star Rating
    measure C36 input.
  - § 4.1 timely filing reject is hard-coded; no exception path
    for member-good-cause requests.
