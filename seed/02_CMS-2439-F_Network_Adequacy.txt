CMS-2439-F · MEDICARE ADVANTAGE NETWORK ADEQUACY AND PROVIDER DIRECTORY ACCURACY FINAL RULE
Issuing body: Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS)
Regulation ID: CMS-2439-F
Effective date: 2026-04-01
Status: Final Rule
Publication: Federal Register, Vol. 90, No. 248


§ 1.0 — Purpose

This Final Rule establishes minimum requirements for provider network
adequacy and the maintenance, accuracy, and public availability of
provider directories for Medicare Advantage organizations. The Rule
responds to documented patterns of "ghost networks" in which listed
providers are unavailable, no longer practicing at the listed
location, or no longer accepting new patients.


§ 2.0 — Scope

This Rule applies to all Medicare Advantage organizations, Special
Needs Plans, and Cost Plans operating under 42 CFR Part 422. It is
binding on contract year 2026 and forward.


§ 3.0 — Definitions

(a) "Provider directory" means any printed, electronic, or telephonic
list of network providers published by or on behalf of a Medicare
Advantage organization, intended for use by enrollees and prospective
enrollees in selecting providers.

(b) "Verified entry" means a directory entry for which the provider
has affirmatively confirmed, within the verification window defined
in § 5.2, that the entry's information remains accurate.

(c) "Time and distance standard" means the maximum travel time or
travel distance from an enrollee's residence to the nearest network
provider of a given specialty, as defined in Appendix A of this Rule.


§ 4.0 — Network Adequacy Requirements

§ 4.1 — Time-and-Distance Standards

Each Medicare Advantage organization shall maintain a provider
network meeting the time-and-distance standards in Appendix A for
each county in the service area, for the following specialty
categories: primary care, cardiology, oncology, behavioral health,
endocrinology, nephrology, and ophthalmology.

§ 4.2 — Behavioral Health Adequacy

For behavioral health specifically, the network shall include not
fewer than one (1) in-network psychiatrist per five thousand (5,000)
enrollees and not fewer than one (1) in-network licensed therapist
or psychologist per two thousand (2,000) enrollees.

§ 4.3 — Annual Network Adequacy Attestation

Each Medicare Advantage organization shall attest to network adequacy
annually no later than January 31, using the prescribed CMS form.


§ 5.0 — Directory Accuracy

§ 5.1 — Verification Window

Each directory entry shall be verified by the listed provider at
least every NINETY (90) days. An entry not verified within the
ninety-day window shall be marked as "unverified" in all
member-facing displays and shall be removed from the directory if
not verified within an additional thirty (30) days.

§ 5.2 — Required Verification Data Elements

Verification shall confirm at minimum:
  (i) provider's current practice location(s);
  (ii) current acceptance of new patients of the relevant LOB;
  (iii) current telephone number for appointment scheduling;
  (iv) current panel size or appointment-availability indicator;
  (v) any languages spoken other than English;
  (vi) wheelchair accessibility and ADA compliance flags.

§ 5.3 — Directory Refresh Cadence

Public-facing provider directories shall be refreshed not less than
once every five (5) business days. The refresh process shall apply
all verification updates received since the prior refresh.


§ 6.0 — Member Notification

§ 6.1 — Material Change Notification

If a network provider is removed from the directory or otherwise
becomes unavailable, members assigned to that provider as primary
care provider, or with an active prior authorization referencing
that provider, shall be notified within ten (10) business days of
the removal.

§ 6.2 — Substitution Right

A member whose primary care provider is removed shall be permitted
to select a replacement provider from the current network at no
out-of-pocket cost, regardless of network-tier differentials.


§ 7.0 — Reporting

§ 7.1 — Quarterly Directory Accuracy Report

Each Medicare Advantage organization shall submit to CMS, within
thirty (30) calendar days of the close of each calendar quarter,
a directory accuracy report containing:
  (i) total directory entries by specialty;
  (ii) verification rate by specialty;
  (iii) member complaints related to directory inaccuracy;
  (iv) corrective actions taken;
  (v) network-adequacy compliance status by county.

§ 7.2 — Member Complaint Tracking

Each Medicare Advantage organization shall maintain a member
complaint log specific to directory inaccuracy, retained for not
less than six (6) years, and shall make it available to CMS on
request.


§ 8.0 — Enforcement

Failure to comply with this Rule may result in:
  (a) civil monetary penalty under 42 CFR § 422.752 up to fifty
      thousand dollars ($50,000) per occurrence for material
      directory inaccuracy resulting in member harm;
  (b) intermediate sanctions including suspension of marketing
      activities and new enrollment;
  (c) corrective action plan with monthly attestations until
      directory verification rate exceeds ninety-five percent (95%);
  (d) referral to the HHS Office of Inspector General for pattern
      violations.
